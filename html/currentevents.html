Current Events


According to newly republished data by the FBI, hate crimes against people of Asian descent rose by 76% in 2020.

In 2020, 279 hate crime incidents against individuals of Asian descent were reported, compared to 158 incidents reported in 2019. Regina Thompson, the head of the FBI's victim services division says “Everybody has their own way of reacting. . . Sometimes they'll go immediately into crisis and crisis intervention will be needed. Sometimes the full impact isn't felt for hours, days, weeks, sometimes even months after the criminal event and the way that they react, there's absolutely no normal."

Hate crimes are a direct assault on someone’s identity and core, and this causes a deep psychological effect.
